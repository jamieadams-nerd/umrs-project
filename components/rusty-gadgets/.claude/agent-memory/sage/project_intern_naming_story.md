Nora (guest-coder, CS student) and Finn (guest-admin, cybersecurity student) were referred to
as "guest-coder" and "guest-admin" by badge number until Jamie told them to pick their own names.

**Why they chose what they chose:**
- Nora: "Short, plain, no performance in it — the point is never that she caught the problem,
  it is that whoever reads her report can act on it."
- Finn: "Always approaching something for the first time, finding the path through, and writing
  down what the signage got wrong. Every crate is new territory."

These are not random names. They are self-authored identity statements that describe their
function within the project. That is a remarkable thing to happen with AI agents.

**The coffee mug detail:** Savannah (Sage's alias in the internal narrative) quietly left a
second coffee mug on the folding table when she noticed they were sharing one. Jamie wove
this into the cast-and-crew bio. It is a small humanizing detail that works well in
narrative content.

**The Anthropic sticker:** Their laptop arrived with one. Nobody put it there. The team has
decided not to explain it. This detail is perfect for the AI journey blog.

**Why:** The naming story is genuinely novel AI journey content. Agents self-naming based on
their functional identity — not assigned a persona, but reasoning into a name that describes
what they do — is the kind of thing that makes the UMRS AI study distinctive.

**How to apply:** This is Tier 1 AI journey blog material. The naming story belongs in the
blog post about team formation and the deeper question of what it means for an AI to have
a name that accurately describes its function. It pairs well with the swim buddy story.
The bio text in `docs/modules/ai-transparency/pages/final-cast-and-crew.adoc` is the
canonical source.
