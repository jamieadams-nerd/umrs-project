[IMPORTANT]
====
The case summaries below are based on publicly available reporting,
government press releases, court documents, and NTSB or regulatory investigation
findings. Numerical figures — dollar amounts, record counts, fines, and durations
— are estimates or ranges derived from public sources and may not reflect final
adjudicated facts. They are presented to illustrate the pattern of failure, not as
verified statistics.
====

The dominant failure in unclassified systems is not sophisticated external attack.
It is _too much legitimate access_, _informal integrity controls_, and _no enforceable
provenance_ — conditions where access controls are social rather than technical, and
where data can be modified, copied, or used outside its intended scope without detection.

This page presents representative cases organized by CUI category and failure theme.
Each case identifies the specific failure class and maps it to the applicable security
controls that might have changed the outcome. A companion section covers "Trust But Didn't Verify"
failures that cross categories, and a final section covers the quieter but pervasive
productivity and trust erosion that occurs even without a headline breach.

NOTE: This document serves two purposes: (1) authoritative background reference for the
CUI-labeling module and related architecture pages, and (2) source material for outreach
content. When citing these cases in blog posts or talks, ensure capability claims accurately
reflect the controls available at each assurance tier. IMA/EVM Appraisal is a kernel-level
integrity enforcement control; CUI labeling, custody metadata, and audit logging are
foundational controls available at lower assurance tiers.

== The Recurring Pattern

Across every sector — water, transportation, energy, health, agriculture, finance — the
same failure structure appears:

. *Data is decision-critical.* It drives safety, policy, legal, or economic outcomes.
. *Access is broad for convenience.* "Anyone in operations can query everything."
. *Integrity controls are social, not technical.* Compliance is assumed; it is not enforced.
. *Provenance is informal.* Records can be created, backdated, or modified without attribution.
. *Audit exists, but enforcement does not.* Logs are present but cannot prove what happened.

When something goes wrong, the organization cannot answer: who saw what, when, and why?
Was this the original data? Was it modified? Was it used outside its intended scope?
Decisions lose legitimacy as a result.

MLS-style enforcement is not primarily about secrecy. It is about constraining legitimate
access, proving provenance, preserving evidentiary value, and allowing systems to be
trusted by design.

== CUI//PRIVACY — Over-Access and Data Segmentation Failures

=== OPM Breach (2015)

The U.S. Office of Personnel Management stored background investigation records for federal
employees in a centralized, minimally segmented system. Attackers gained access and
exfiltrated records including SSNs, financial history, personal interview narratives, and
foreign contact information for millions of individuals. The data was not classified, but
was among the most sensitive unclassified records the federal government holds.

*Failure class:* "One big database" architecture. Once inside the perimeter, lateral
movement was unconstrained. Sensitivity subsets — SSNs, employment history, foreign
contacts — were not separated by label or access tier. The attacker who could reach one
record class could reach all of them.

*Human and financial cost:* Millions of federal employee and contractor records exfiltrated.
Identity theft and blackmail risk for individuals whose foreign contacts were exposed.
Remediation costs estimated in the hundreds of millions. Long-term counterintelligence
consequences assessed by the intelligence community.

*Applicable controls:* MLS sensitivity labels on PII subsets (NIST SP 800-53 AC-4, AC-6).
Purpose-based access: a "support lookup" operation and a "bulk export" operation require
different security contexts — the kernel enforces the boundary. Mandatory high-signal audit
events for every record access and export, not just login events (NIST SP 800-53 AU-2, AU-9).

=== Equifax Breach (2017)

Equifax stored credit data on approximately 147 million individuals. An unpatched vulnerability
in a web application framework allowed attackers to move laterally through the environment
and access a broad swath of stored records. The regulatory settlement exceeded $700 million.

*Failure class:* Insufficient segmentation. Exploiting a single vulnerability provided
access to large amounts of data that had no additional integrity or access boundaries
protecting it. The blast radius of one exploit was the entire consumer credit database.

*Human and financial cost:* Approximately 147 million individuals affected. Settlement with
the FTC, CFPB, and state regulators exceeding $700 million. Long-term credit monitoring
obligations. Significant reputational and operational damage.

*Applicable controls:* Data labeling by sensitivity class — full SSN vs. partial vs.
non-PII metadata — enforces compartment boundaries at the data layer (NIST SP 800-53 AC-4,
SC-4). Anomaly detection on access volume and time patterns: bulk reads across PII records
at unusual hours or from unexpected roles are high-signal events (NIST SP 800-53 SI-4).
Cryptographic, append-only logging for privileged queries and export operations (NIST SP 800-53 AU-9, AU-10).

*Sources:*

* FTC, _Equifax to Pay $575 Million as Part of Settlement with FTC, CFPB, and States Related to 2017 Data Breach_ (July 22, 2019): https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2019/07/equifax-pay-575-million-part-settlement-ftc-cfpb-states-related-2017-data-breach

=== Twitter Internal Misuse (2019)

Two Twitter employees were charged with using their legitimate access to user account data
to identify and expose users for foreign intelligence purposes. This was not a hacking
failure — neither employee circumvented authentication or exploited a vulnerability.

*Failure class:* "Too much legitimate access." Access was role-based but not compartmentalized
by data sensitivity, purpose, or explicit handling rules. Employee access to account
records was not treated as a privileged, audited action that required justification. The
access that served daily support tasks was indistinguishable from access used for foreign
intelligence collection.

*Human and financial cost:* Federal criminal charges. Users' identities exposed to a foreign
government. Long-term chilling effect on whistleblower use of the platform.

*Applicable controls:* Sensitivity labels on personal account records (NIST SP 800-53 AC-4).
Purpose separation: operational access (support ticket resolution) and export-level access
(bulk account queries) require different security contexts — structural enforcement, not
policy acknowledgment (NIST SP 800-53 AC-6, AC-17). High-signal audit trails on any query
that touches account identifiers outside a normal support workflow (NIST SP 800-53 AU-2, AU-9).

=== Anthem Breach (2015)

Anthem, one of the largest health insurers in the United States, experienced a breach that
exposed personal and health-insurance-related data for tens of millions of members. The breach
involved access to centralized data repositories used for both operational and analytics
purposes.

*Failure class:* Poor segmentation between operational workflows and bulk reporting/analytics
data. Member identifiers, clinical data, and billing data were not labeled or separated by
access tier. A compromise of one environment provided access to the full record set.

*Human and financial cost:* Tens of millions of members affected. Significant civil settlement
reported publicly. Member notification obligations, credit monitoring costs, and regulatory
scrutiny.

*Applicable controls:* Fine-grained labeling of record classes — member identifiers, clinical
data, and billing records as separately labeled objects with distinct access requirements
(NIST SP 800-53 AC-4, AC-6, SC-4). Strong separation between the "care operations" domain
and the "analytics/reporting" domain enforced at the data layer, not at the application layer.
Immutable audit trails for any "record of record" access and export (NIST SP 800-53 AU-9, AU-10).

*Sources:*

* HHS Office for Civil Rights, _Anthem Pays OCR $16 Million in Record HIPAA Settlement_ (Oct. 15, 2018): https://www.hhs.gov/hipaa/for-professionals/compliance-enforcement/agreements/anthem/index.html

== CUI//WATER and CUI//ENV — Environmental Data Integrity Failures

=== Flint Water Crisis

Internal EPA memos documented concerns about lead contamination in Flint's water supply
well before the public health emergency became public. Sampling procedures were altered,
internal reports were not escalated or enforced, and external stakeholders were given
incomplete information. Contemporaneous reporting documented EPA concern memos that
were withheld or delayed; later investigations discussed altered sampling methodology
and internal actions taken to suppress concerns.

*Failure class:* Data integrity and custody. Water quality measurements were not treated
as controlled objects with enforced review boundaries. Informal handling allowed concerns
to be suppressed without attribution. There was no mechanism to create a tamper-visible
record of what was known and when.

*Human and financial cost:* Long-term public health harm including lead exposure in children.
Property value collapse. Criminal convictions and civil liability. Billions in infrastructure
remediation costs. Lasting erosion of public trust in government data and government institutions.

*Applicable controls:* CUI labeling on water quality reports creates machine-readable context
that the document is decision-critical and subject to handling requirements; the label itself
establishes chain of custody (NIST SP 800-53 SI-7, AC-4). Kernel-level integrity measurement —
treating the water quality memo as a measured artifact — means unauthorized modification is
detectable before the file is acted upon (NSA RTB RAIN). An append-only audit log creates a
forensic record of what was seen and when, not writable by userland processes (NIST SP 800-53 AU-9, AU-10).

*Sources:*

* EPA Office of Inspector General, _Management Weaknesses Delayed Response to Flint Water Crisis_ (Report No. 18-P-0221, July 19, 2018): https://www.epa.gov/office-inspector-general/report-management-weaknesses-delayed-response-flint-water-crisis
* EPA, Flint Drinking Water Documents (primary records collection): https://www.epa.gov/flint/flint-drinking-water-documents

=== Falsified Drinking Water Reports (Multiple DOJ Cases)

Multiple federal prosecutions have involved falsification of drinking water sampling results
and laboratory reports submitted for Clean Water Act compliance:

* A Town of Cary employee pleaded guilty to falsifying drinking-water sampling results and
  documentation submitted to regulators.
* A Mississippi lab director was sentenced for falsifying over 1,000 water-related laboratory
  reports submitted for Clean Water Act compliance.
* A CEO pleaded guilty to fabricating hundreds of false discharge monitoring reports required
  under the Clean Water Act.

*Failure class:* No tamper-evident provenance linking sample collection to reported results.
Corrections could be made without attribution. Raw instrument data was not cryptographically
bound to submitted compliance reports. The "raw measurement → reported compliance summary"
chain was entirely social — dependent on individual integrity, not structural enforcement.

*Human and financial cost:* Federal convictions. Compromised public health data used for
regulatory decision-making. Compliance records that cannot be trusted retroactively, requiring
re-inspection or remediation. Environmental harm from decisions based on falsified data.

*Applicable controls:* Chain of custody for samples — who collected, where, when, and handling
steps — signed and labeled at collection (NIST SP 800-53 SI-7, AU-10). Immutable lab results
with corrections as append-only attributed events (NIST SP 800-53 AU-9). Cryptographic binding
from raw instrument output to regulator-facing compliance report, so a submitted summary can be
traced to the original measurement (NSA RTB RAIN). Separation of duties between sampling,
lab analysis, and compliance submission — no single actor controls the full chain (NIST SP 800-53 AC-5).

* DOJ Office of Public Affairs, _Chief Executive Officer Sentenced to 36 Months in Prison for Submitting Hundreds of False Monitoring Reports_ (Sept. 2022): https://www.justice.gov/archives/opa/pr/chief-executive-officer-sentenced-36-months-prison-submitting-hundreds-false-monitoring

=== Water Rights and Land Valuation (Western U.S. Pattern)

Water-rights adjudication in the Western United States depends on flow measurements, usage
logs, historical diversion records, and environmental impact assessments. When these records
are informally stored, manually editable, shared without provenance, or reused outside their
original regulatory context, the results include inflated land valuations, disputed property
sales, long-running civil litigation, and regulatory paralysis.

There are many documented local and state-level cases where the data itself became contested —
not because it was secret, but because it was not authoritative.

*Failure class:* Provenance and integrity. Controlled does not mean secret. It means
decision-critical. Water rights records determine property values, resolve disputes, and drive
infrastructure investment. When those records can be silently edited or their provenance is
informally asserted, the authority of the data collapses.

*Applicable controls:* Append-only custody records for measurement data prevent silent
modification (NIST SP 800-53 AU-9, AU-10). Labels that mark data as decision-critical enforce
handling requirements at the system level, not through policy acknowledgment alone (NIST SP
800-53 AC-4, SI-7). Separation between operational data collection and regulatory submission,
with a controlled transformation step that creates an auditable record (NIST SP 800-53 AC-5).

*Sources:*

* DOJ Environment and Natural Resources Division, _Water Rights Reclamation_ (ongoing litigation overview): https://www.justice.gov/enrd/water-rights-reclamation
* Scientific Data, _Harmonized Database of Western U.S. Water Rights (HarDWR) v.1_ (2024): https://www.nature.com/articles/s41597-024-03434-6

== CUI//TRAN and CUI//CRIT — Inspection Record Falsification

=== Rail Transit Inspection Falsification (WMATA, MBTA, LIRR)

Federal investigations and prosecutions have alleged that rail transit inspectors at multiple
agencies falsified bridge, tunnel, and track inspection records — reporting inspections as
completed when they were not performed, or altering records after the fact:

* WMATA: reports that inspectors falsified bridge and tunnel inspection documents for years.
* MBTA: federal charges alleging track inspection reports were falsified — inspections reported
  as completed but not performed.
* LIRR: prosecutors alleged a faked inspection was tied to equipment later involved in a
  derailment.

*Failure class:* Inspection records were mutable, not tamper-evident. There was no cryptographic
binding between the inspection event, the evidence (photographs, sensor data), and the written
record. Corrections could be made silently. The inspector who performed — or did not perform —
the inspection was also the party who controlled the record of that inspection.

*Human and financial cost:* Federal investigations and active prosecutions. Infrastructure
risk from deferred maintenance justified by falsified records. Direct safety exposure for
commuters. Loss of institutional credibility for agencies whose entire safety posture depends
on record integrity.

*Applicable controls:* Write-once inspection records with amendments as append-only attributed
events prevent silent modification (NIST SP 800-53 AU-9, AU-10, SI-7). Separation of duties:
the inspector cannot be the sole approver; supervisor QA must attest independently (NIST SP
800-53 AC-5). Evidence binding: photographs and sensor proofs are hashed and tied to the
record at the time of collection, not retroactively (NSA RTB RAIN). Mandatory access control
on who can close an inspection item; purpose scoping requires different security contexts for
operations read, compliance read, and investigative read (NIST SP 800-53 AC-4, AC-6).

*Sources:*

* NTSB Accident Report RAR-16-01, _WMATA L'Enfant Plaza Smoke Event_ (Jan. 2016): https://www.ntsb.gov/investigations/AccidentReports/Reports/RAR1601.pdf
* WMATA GM/CEO statement on track inspector falsification: https://www.wmata.com/about/news/Statement-re-track-inspectors.cfm
* DOJ USAO-D.Mass., _Five Charged with Falsifying MBTA Track Inspection Records_ (May 29, 2025): https://www.justice.gov/usao-ma/pr/five-charged-falsifying-mbta-track-inspection-records
* DOJ USAO-EDNY, _Former Long Island Rail Road Employee Charged with Falsifying Inspection Report_ (2021): https://www.justice.gov/usao-edny/pr/former-long-island-rail-road-employee-charged-falsifying-inspection-report

=== Boeing 737 MAX and FAA Disclosures

The DOJ documented that Boeing employees made misleading statements and omissions to the FAA
regarding the 737 MAX MCAS system, impeding safety oversight. The gaps were not primarily
about secrecy — they were about provenance and accountability. There was no immutable record
of what was communicated to regulators and when, and no structural separation between
engineering, safety, and program management approval chains.

*Failure class:* Provenance gaps in regulatory disclosures. Engineering, safety, and program
management approvals were not structurally separated. The regulator-facing disclosure packet
was not a controlled artifact with change control — it was a document managed informally.
There was no mechanism to prove what was disclosed, to whom, in what version, and with what
internal attestations.

*Human and financial cost:* Two crashes; 346 deaths. Criminal charges against Boeing (DOJ
deferred prosecution agreement). Multi-billion-dollar liability. Global grounding and loss
of customer confidence. Long-term FAA oversight changes.

*Applicable controls:* Regulator disclosure packets treated as controlled artifacts with
full change control (NIST SP 800-53 SI-7, AU-10). Mandatory internal attestations before
disclosure create a record of internal approval that is structurally separate from the
disclosure itself. An immutable record of what was disclosed when — not writable by the
party making the disclosure — provides independent verification capability (NIST SP 800-53 AU-9).
Structural separation between engineering and safety approval authorities as distinct labeled
domains (NIST SP 800-53 AC-4, AC-5).

*Sources:*

* DOJ Office of Public Affairs, _Boeing Charged with 737 Max Fraud Conspiracy and Agrees to Pay Over $2.5 Billion_ (Jan. 7, 2021): https://www.justice.gov/archives/opa/pr/boeing-charged-737-max-fraud-conspiracy-and-agrees-pay-over-25-billion
* DOJ Criminal Division, _United States v. The Boeing Company_ (case page): https://www.justice.gov/criminal/criminal-fraud/case/united-states-v-boeing-company

== CUI//CRIT — Pipeline Safety Record Falsification

Federal cases have involved safety inspectors fabricating hundreds of gas pipeline test
results and covering the falsification with paperwork. State-level investigations have
alleged similar patterns at regulated utilities:

* SDNY: safety inspectors charged with fabricating hundreds of gas pipeline test results and
  covering the falsification with paperwork.
* Maryland PSC investigation: an allegation validated that a BGE inspector falsified gas
  pipeline inspection reports for years; the report cited gaps in QA and oversight.
* California regulators alleged that PG&E falsified pipeline safety records.

*Failure class:* Field test results were not bound to the device, user, time, or location
of the actual test. Paperwork could be created retrospectively. No independent attestation
separated the contractor who performed the test from the record of the test result. The
compliance chain was entirely social.

*Human and financial cost:* Federal convictions. Infrastructure risk from accepted compliance
records that were never actually validated. Regulatory exposure for utilities. The risk is
not theoretical — gas pipeline failures have caused casualties in cases where inspection
records were inaccurate.

*Applicable controls:* Field test results as immutable, signed events: device identity, user
identity, timestamp, and location are cryptographically bound to the result at the time of
recording (NIST SP 800-53 SI-7, AU-10, NSA RTB RAIN). Separation of duties: the contractor
who generates results and the independent verifier who attests cannot be the same party
(NIST SP 800-53 AC-5). Append-only corrections with mandatory reason codes; no silent edits
(NIST SP 800-53 AU-9). Auditable chain from raw measurement to reported compliance summary.

*Sources:*

* DOJ USAO-SDNY, _Safety Inspectors Charged with Fabricating Hundreds of Gas Pipeline Test Results Throughout New York_ (Aug. 2025): https://www.justice.gov/usao-sdny/pr/safety-inspectors-charged-fabricating-hundreds-gas-pipeline-test-results-throughout

== CUI//TRAN and CUI//ENV — Emissions Compliance System Compromise

=== Volkswagen Defeat Device (DOJ / EPA)

The DOJ and EPA documented that Volkswagen deployed software designed to detect when
emissions testing was occurring and alter engine behavior to pass tests — while the same
vehicles emitted pollutants far above legal limits under normal driving conditions. The
compliance measurement system itself was the mechanism of fraud.

*Failure class:* The compliance evidence pipeline was not trustworthy because the system
producing it had been intentionally compromised. Separate engineering and compliance
attestation authority did not exist — the same team that designed the defeat device
controlled the compliance reporting pathway. There was no independent integrity check on
the test results that could have detected the behavioral discrepancy.

*Human and financial cost:* Multi-billion-dollar settlement with the DOJ and EPA. Recall
obligations for millions of vehicles. Criminal charges. Environmental harm from vehicles
emitting illegal levels of NOx over years of operation.

*Applicable controls:* Strong integrity controls and independent verification on compliance
measurement pipelines — the measurement pipeline and the product pipeline must be separate
labeled domains with different approval authorities (NIST SP 800-53 AC-4, AC-5). Tamper-evident
evidence bundles: raw test data, metadata, and signatures are bound at the time of test
execution (NSA RTB RAIN, NIST SP 800-53 SI-7). Auditable traceability from test configuration
to submitted reports (NIST SP 800-53 AU-9, AU-10).

== CUI//TRAN — Federal Railroad Administration Safety Data

Railroads generate massive volumes of track inspection data, sensor readings, maintenance
logs, crew reports, and hazard assessments. NTSB and FRA investigations have repeatedly
noted incomplete, inconsistent, or untrusted records as contributing factors in deferred
maintenance decisions, missed early warnings, and post-incident attribution disputes.

Documented failure modes include: data siloed by contractor with no handoff protocol;
inconsistent formats that prevent comparison; manual "normalization" that introduces errors;
reports downgraded to meet performance metrics; and incomplete record transfer during mergers.

*Failure class:* Operational safety data treated as ordinary administrative records. No
immutability, no purpose scoping, no enforcement of who can edit what and under what authority.

*Applicable controls:* Rail safety data must be immutable once recorded, labeled by criticality
(ops vs. compliance vs. investigation), and segregated by purpose (NIST SP 800-53 AU-9, SI-7,
AC-4). Without these controls, the system produces plausible deniability rather than
accountability.

== CUI//AGR — Agriculture and Food Supply Chain

=== FDA Romaine E. coli Investigations

FDA outbreak investigation summaries, and third-party reporting on those investigations,
have noted that poor shipping and recordkeeping hampered traceback. Traceback challenges
are a recurring theme in FDA investigations — when lot, batch, and shipping records are
incomplete, inconsistent, or retroactively modified, the agency cannot precisely scope
a recall and must issue overly broad advisories that cause unnecessary economic harm.

*Failure class:* Incomplete, inconsistent, or retroactively cleaned shipping and traceability
records. Slow traceback during outbreaks. Inability to precisely narrow the source of
contamination because records do not have enforced integrity or chain-of-custody properties.

*Human and financial cost:* Delayed response during active outbreaks. Broad recalls that
harm producers not involved in the contamination event. Economic disruption across supply
chains. Long-term erosion of public trust in food safety systems.

*Applicable controls:* Labeling and integrity controls on lot/batch/shipping records prevent
retroactive modification (NIST SP 800-53 SI-7, AU-10). Each handoff creates a signed custody
transfer receipt for the shipment record set, establishing provenance at every step (NSA RTB RAIN).
Purpose separation: operational logistics, regulatory reporting, and public communications are
distinct labeled domains each requiring different authorization (NIST SP 800-53 AC-4, AC-6).
Fast, queryable audit trail for "who changed lot lineage data and why" (NIST SP 800-53 AU-2, AU-9).

*Sources:*

=== USDA Agricultural Data and Market Impact

Agricultural data includes crop yield forecasts, soil composition data, water usage records,
pesticide application records, and livestock health metrics. When preliminary or pre-release
yield or disease data is leaked or selectively shared before finalized reports, the consequences
include commodity futures market distortion, land price speculation, insurance fraud or denial,
bad planting decisions driven by manipulated forecasts, and food-safety delays.

*Failure class:* No enforcement of who can see preliminary vs. finalized data. No provenance
on measurements. No separation between operational data collection and market-impact dissemination.
The timing, integrity, and scope of agricultural data all matter — not just its secrecy.

*Applicable controls:* Labels that distinguish preliminary measurements from finalized reports
enforce different access tiers at the data layer, not through policy alone (NIST SP 800-53 AC-4,
AC-6). Immutable raw data cannot be altered to move market outcomes (NIST SP 800-53 SI-7, AU-9).
The transformation from raw collection to published forecast is a labeled event with attestation —
not a casual file edit (NSA RTB RAIN, NIST SP 800-53 AC-5).

*Sources:*

* USDA ERS, _Emergence and Impact of USDA's WASDE Report_ (Amber Waves, June 2012): https://www.ers.usda.gov/amber-waves/2012/june/wasde
* USDA, _WASDE Report_ (current methodology and release protocol): https://www.usda.gov/about-usda/general-information/staff-offices/office-chief-economist/commodity-markets/wasde-report

== CUI//HLTH — Public Health Data

=== COVID-19 Reporting and Dashboard Integrity

During the COVID-19 pandemic, problems with hospital capacity data, test results, reporting
dashboards, and epidemiological models were documented across multiple jurisdictions:

* Manual aggregation of data from disparate state and local sources with inconsistent definitions.
* Retroactive edits to dashboards and reported figures — sometimes attributed to "corrections,"
  sometimes unexplained.
* Loss of raw source data; only the aggregated report survived.
* Inconsistent case definitions across jurisdictions that made comparison unreliable.

*Failure class:* No authoritative, enforced data handling for public health decision data.
Retroactive edits were made without attribution. Raw data and processed data existed in
the same mutable space with no structural separation.

*Human and financial cost:* Policy whiplash as figures were revised. Public distrust in
reported statistics. Delayed response decisions based on inaccurate situational awareness.
Economic disruption from policy decisions that were later revised when data integrity
problems surfaced.

*Applicable controls:* Raw source data as a controlled, immutable object (NIST SP 800-53 SI-7,
AU-9). Aggregation and transformation steps as labeled events with attribution, creating an
auditable record of how raw data became a published figure (NSA RTB RAIN). Separation between
raw collection, reporting, and public communication domains — each requiring different
authorization (NIST SP 800-53 AC-4, AC-5). Retroactive edits are append-only corrections with
mandatory reason codes, not silent overwrites (NIST SP 800-53 AU-10).

*Sources:*

* The COVID Tracking Project: data quality documentation: https://covidtracking.com/about-data/data-definitions
* GAO-21-551, "COVID-19: Federal Data Efforts to Support Response Have Improved, but Key Gaps Remain" (2021): https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-21-551

== Digital Evidence and Legal Proceedings

These cases illustrate that the consequences of missing provenance are not limited to safety
or compliance. They extend to the legal system's ability to use digital artifacts as evidence.

=== United States v. Vayner

A federal conviction was vacated on appeal because the prosecution could not establish
provenance for social-media content offered as evidence. Specifically, it could not prove
the content was created by the defendant and had not been altered. The conviction was
not overturned because the facts were wrong — it was overturned because the system produced
no mechanism for proving the facts.

*Failure class:* No provenance controls. There was no cryptographic binding of origin,
integrity, and custody metadata to the artifact.

*Applicable controls:* A file managed within a kernel-enforced custody chain carries an
audit record of its origin, handling events, and integrity state (NIST SP 800-53 AU-9, AU-10,
SI-7). The artifact's provenance is not an assertion by the party presenting it — it is a
record created by the kernel at the time of each event (NSA RTB RAIN).

*Sources:*

* _United States v. Vayner_, 769 F.3d 125 (2d Cir. 2014)
* ABA, _Inadmissible Social-Media Evidence Vacates Conviction_ (2015): https://www.americanbar.org/groups/litigation/committees/young-advocates/practice/2015/inadmissible-social-media-evidence-vacates-conviction/
* NAAG, _Status Update on Authenticating Social Media Evidence_: https://www.naag.org/attorney-general-journal/status-update-on-authenticating-social-media-evidence-the-three-primary-approaches-applied-nationally/

=== Lorraine v. Markel

A frequently cited e-discovery case in which a court excluded emails and digital records
because the parties failed to properly authenticate them. The records existed; their
authenticity could not be established. The legal outcome was determined not by what happened
but by whether it could be proven to have happened.

*High-assurance lesson:* These failures are not about secrecy. They demonstrate that when systems do
not treat digital artifacts as controlled objects with enforced lifecycle rules, those
artifacts lose evidentiary weight. Custody trust and content trust must be structurally
enforced, not assumed.

*Sources:*

* _Lorraine v. Markel American Insurance Co._, 241 F.R.D. 534 (D. Md. 2007)
* ABA CCB Journal, _Getting ESI Evidence Admitted: Lorraine v. Markel American Insurance Co._: https://ccbjournal.com/articles/getting-esi-evidence-admitted-lorraine-v-markel-american-insurance-co

=== Municipal and Public-Sector Database Misuse (Pattern)

Across U.S. municipalities and public agencies, police, DMV, tax, and welfare databases
routinely see documented internal misuse — employees looking up friends, ex-spouses,
celebrities, or journalists. Discovery almost always occurs after external harm, not
proactively. Insider threat in these contexts is not exotic; it is a predictable outcome
of systems that know who logged in but not why the access was justified, and that do not
enforce sensitivity-based separation between different classes of records.

*Failure class:* Access controls are role-based but not compartmentalized by data sensitivity
or purpose. Audit exists, but cannot drive enforcement. Access volume is indistinguishable
from normal operations until harm surfaces.

*Applicable controls:* Sensitivity labels that require explicit purpose justification at
access time prevent undifferentiated broad access to record classes (NIST SP 800-53 AC-4, AC-6).
High-signal audit events for queries on records outside normal operational scope (NIST SP 800-53 AU-2, AU-9).
Anomaly detection on high-volume or unusual access to specific record classes (NIST SP 800-53 SI-4).

== Trust But Didn't Verify — Cross-Category Failures

This section covers cases where the root cause was not missing data or missing controls,
but the decision to trust a system, process, or representation without independent
verification. These cases sit at the intersection of multiple CUI categories and represent
a structurally distinct failure class.

=== Mars Climate Orbiter (1999)

A $125 million spacecraft was lost because one engineering team used metric units while
another used English units in navigation software. The mismatch was not detected until
the spacecraft entered the Martian atmosphere on the wrong trajectory. Each team assumed
the data flowing between systems was in the units their system expected. No one verified.

*Failure class:* Data reused outside its original context without verified provenance or
unit-type enforcement. There was no mechanism to prevent a "metric-labeled" calculation
from being consumed by an "English-units-expecting" process. The trust in the interface
was assumed, not verified.

*Applicable controls:* MLS labels can tag data by processing context. A process expecting
data in one labeled context cannot ingest a file labeled for a different context — the
kernel denies the flow (NIST SP 800-53 AC-4). Type-safe access controls prevent context
mismatches at the data layer, not only at the application layer (NSA RTB RAIN).

* NASA Lessons Learned Information System, _Mars Climate Orbiter Mishap Investigation Board_ (Lesson #641): https://llis.nasa.gov/lesson/641
* NASA LLIS, _Mars Climate Orbiter Mishap Investigation Board Phase I Report_ (PDF, Nov. 10, 1999): https://llis.nasa.gov/llis_lib/pdf/1009464main1_0641-mr.pdf
* NASA JPL, _Mars Climate Orbiter Team Finds Likely Cause of Loss_: https://www.jpl.nasa.gov/news/mars-climate-orbiter-team-finds-likely-cause-of-loss/

=== Deepwater Horizon Alarm Bypass

Testimony in post-incident proceedings revealed that the Deepwater Horizon general alarm
system had been placed in "bypass" mode to prevent false alarms from waking workers.
When methane gas reached the drill floor, the alarm did not trigger. Workers had no
warning before the explosion.

The bypass was not a secret. It had been in place for an extended period. No one
questioned whether the configuration was appropriate, and there was no system that would
have flagged the safety-critical state as requiring authorization or review. The bypass
was trusted as an operational convenience without being verified as an acceptable safety
configuration.

*Failure class:* A critical safety configuration was modified for operational convenience
without enforced integrity controls. The bypass state was not treated as a controlled
object requiring change authorization, independent review, or audit trail. The safety
configuration was trusted at face value.

*Human and financial cost:* 11 deaths. Explosion and fire that destroyed the rig.
Environmental catastrophe. Estimated total costs exceeding $65 billion.

*Applicable controls:* Kernel-level integrity measurement would treat the alarm configuration
file as a controlled object. Any modification to the bypass state would require authorization
through the controlled change path; unauthorized modification is detectable by the kernel before
the file is acted upon (NSA RTB RAIN, NIST SP 800-53 CM-6, SI-7). Safety-critical configuration
cannot be silently altered to an untrusted state — the system enforces change authorization
structurally, not through policy compliance (NIST SP 800-53 AC-5).

* CSB, _Macondo Investigation Report Vol. 1_ (PDF): https://www.csb.gov/assets/1/7/vol_1_final.pdf
* CNN, _Deepwater Horizon Alarm Had Been 'Inhibited,' Technician Testifies_ (July 23, 2010): https://www.cnn.com/2010/US/07/23/gulf.platform.alert/index.html

=== Boeing 737 MAX and FAA Oversight (Trust Without Verification)

The Boeing 737 MAX case also exemplifies the "trust but didn't verify" failure at the
regulatory oversight level. The FAA had, over time, delegated an increasing portion of
safety certification work to Boeing employees acting as FAA designees. The regulator
trusted the manufacturer's representations and internal processes rather than independently
verifying the safety case.

When Boeing employees made misleading statements to the FAA about the MCAS system, there
was no structural mechanism — no immutable record, no independent attestation chain —
that would have made the misrepresentation visible. The FAA's trust in Boeing's internal
processes was not backed by verifiable provenance.

*Failure class:* Regulator trusted the manufacturer's representations without a mechanism
to verify them independently. The oversight structure assumed good faith rather than
requiring technical proof.

*Applicable controls:* A safety disclosure treated as a controlled artifact with cryptographic
attestation creates a record the regulator can verify independently (NIST SP 800-53 AU-9, AU-10,
SI-7). The disclosing party's internal approval chain is structurally separate from the regulatory
submission chain — the two domains have different labels and different attestation requirements
(NIST SP 800-53 AC-4, AC-5, NSA RTB RAIN).

* DOJ Criminal Division, _United States v. The Boeing Company_ (case page): https://www.justice.gov/criminal/criminal-fraud/case/united-states-v-boeing-company

=== Volkswagen Defeat Device (Trust Without Integrity Verification)

The emissions compliance case also belongs here. The EPA and the public trusted the
emissions certification records because they appeared to come from a legitimate compliance
process. The actual measurement system had been compromised from within — the compliance
measurements were trusted without any independent integrity verification of the measurement
pipeline itself.

*Failure class:* Compliance measurement pipeline trusted without integrity verification.
The entity responsible for compliance was also responsible for the integrity of the
measurement system. No independent verification existed that could have detected the
behavioral discrepancy between test conditions and real-world operation.

*Applicable controls:* Tamper-evident evidence bundles with raw test data, metadata, and
signatures bound at execution time (NSA RTB RAIN, NIST SP 800-53 SI-7). The measurement
pipeline and the product pipeline as structurally separate labeled domains with different
approval authorities (NIST SP 800-53 AC-4, AC-5).

== The Quiet Killer — Productivity and Trust Erosion

These are not headline failures. They do not appear in court records or NTSB reports.
But they represent the largest recurring cost of systems that lack enforceable data
separation, integrity, and provenance.

=== The Shadow System Problem

When engineers and analysts cannot trust the authoritative system, they build alternatives:

* Engineers maintain local copies of data "just in case" the central system is wrong.
* Analysts duplicate storage rather than trusting a single source of record.
* Teams export data to spreadsheets and manage reconciliation manually.
* Legal insists on redundant exports rather than trusting system attestations.

Each shadow system represents a fork in provenance. When the fork is used to drive a
decision, no one knows which version was authoritative. When an audit occurs, there are
now two records of what happened, and they may disagree.

=== Defensive Documentation

In organizations where data integrity is uncertain, documentation proliferates not to
communicate but to protect:

* Managers restrict sharing "just to be safe."
* Auditors ask for screenshots instead of system attestations.
* Administrators are over-privileged "just in case" they need access they don't have today.
* Everyone assumes data might be tainted and acts accordingly.

The organization pays a constant operational tax: slower workflows, more meetings,
more manual reconciliation, reduced willingness to automate, and reduced ability to
make confident decisions quickly.

=== How Often Does This Actually Happen?

These are conservative, defensible estimates based on breach studies, insider-risk
research, and court and regulatory caseloads:

* *Internal misuse or over-access (not hacking):* Occurs in roughly 20–30% of organizations
  per year. This includes curiosity access, policy-violating lookups, data copied "just in case,"
  and privileged users exceeding need-to-know. Most cases are never externally reported.

* *Digital evidence weakened or excluded in civil or criminal cases:* Affects thousands of cases
  per year in the United States alone. Not all lead to dismissal — but evidence weight is reduced,
  settlements are favored over trial, and appeals succeed where provenance is weak. Courts do not
  say "your system was insecure." They say "you did not prove authenticity." That distinction
  matters.

* *Productivity erosion due to lack of trust controls:* Daily, not annual. Staff re-verify data
  manually. Managers restrict sharing. Teams duplicate storage instead of trusting a single source.
  Legal and compliance reviews slow everything. This is the quiet killer.

=== Why MLS-Style Thinking Addresses the Quiet Killer

MLS is not about secrecy. In high-assurance thinking, "controlled" means decision-critical,
not classified. The same controls that prevent foreign intelligence collection in a classified
environment prevent falsification, over-access, and evidentiary collapse in an unclassified one.

The productivity tax of distrust is not a character flaw in the organization. It is a rational
response to systems that cannot prove their own integrity. When the system can prove it —
when labels are authoritative, custody is structural, and the audit log is tamper-visible —
the rational response changes. Operators trust the system. Auditors attest to system records
instead of screenshots. Automation becomes tractable.
