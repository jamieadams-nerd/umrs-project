= CUI Category Descriptions (UMRS Mapping)
:toc: left
:description: Definitions and handling restrictions for CUI categories as mapped to UMRS sensitivity levels c0 through c9.

// NIST 800-171 Rev 2 — 3.1.3 Control CUI flow
// NIST 800-53 AC-4 — Information flow enforcement
// EO 13556 — Controlled Unclassified Information

This page describes the CUI categories used in the UMRS sensitivity level
mapping (c0 through c9) with their definitions and access handling restrictions.
These descriptions are paraphrased from NARA CUI Registry guidance.

For the full registry of DoD CUI abbreviations, see
xref:cui-category-abbreviations.adoc[CUI category abbreviations].

== Category Definitions

=== c0 — CUI (General)

The baseline category for all Controlled Unclassified Information. Indicates
that information requires controlled handling but does not specify a specialized
subcategory.

*Handling:* Must be safeguarded from unauthorized access and shared only with
authorized personnel who have a lawful government purpose. Requires access control
and proper dissemination markings. Must not be posted publicly. No encryption
requirement beyond standard organizational policy.

=== c1 — PRIVACY (PII)

Personally Identifiable Information. Information that can be used to distinguish or
trace an individual's identity, either alone or combined with other personal or
identifying information.

*Handling:* Must be protected from inadvertent exposure. Requires privacy-protective
handling, limited disclosure, and shredding/disposal rules. Should be encrypted at
rest and in transit. Strict need-to-know basis.

=== c2 — TAX

Federal tax information regulated under the Internal Revenue Code (IRC). Includes
returns, return information, and tax-related data protected by statute.

*Handling:* Disclosure prohibitions under 26 USC 6103. Requires strong access control,
strict audit logging, approved destruction methods, and may require encryption in
transit and at rest.

=== c3 — EXPORT

Information requiring protection under export control regulations, including ITAR
(International Traffic in Arms Regulations) and EAR (Export Administration
Regulations).

*Handling:* Must not be shared with foreign nationals or exported without authorization.
Requires strict access control, need-to-know enforcement, possible segmentation or
isolation, and auditability. Handling may be subject to criminal penalties for
violations.

=== c4 — CONTRACT

Controlled information required to be protected by government contract clauses.
Includes proprietary contractor data.

*Handling:* Must be protected under DFARS, FAR, and acquisition-related clauses.
Requires limited dissemination, proper storage, protection from unauthorized release,
and adherence to contract-specific controls.

=== c5 — LEGAL

Attorney-client privileged information, litigation information, or information
protected under legal discovery privilege.

*Handling:* Must be restricted to legal personnel or individuals with explicit
authorization. Requires tight access control and audit trail maintenance. Must not
be shared outside the authorized legal channel.

=== c6 — FINANCIAL

Sensitive financial information including audits, budget details, procurement data,
internal financial controls, or data protected under financial statutes.

*Handling:* Requires restricted access, protection from tampering, and
integrity-preserving storage. Often requires audit logging and controlled
dissemination.

=== c7 — PROCUREMENT

Information related to acquisition planning, source selection, contract evaluation,
bid materials, or vendor-sensitive submissions.

*Handling:* Must not be released to unauthorized bidders or external parties. Requires
confidentiality protections, access logging, and strict segregation during acquisition
activities.

=== c8 — TECHNICAL

Controlled technical information including system specifications, engineering data,
schematics, research results, and technical documentation protected under law or
regulation.

*Handling:* Must be protected against unauthorized export, copying, or distribution.
Requires integrity controls, access restrictions, and possibly encryption depending
on organizational requirements.

=== c9 — INFRA

Critical infrastructure information including systems, facilities, operational
dependencies, or vulnerability data related to infrastructure sectors.

*Handling:* Requires strong access control, prohibition of public release, secure
storage, and reporting rules if compromised. May require encryption in transit and
at rest.
