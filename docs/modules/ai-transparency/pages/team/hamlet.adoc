== Hamlet
_Changelog Updater_

[cols="1,2", frame=none, grid=none]
|===
a|image::hamlet.png[Hamlet, 200, align=center]
a|
Hamlet is the team pet. He is a hamster. This is not a
metaphor. He runs on a wheel — literally, a steampunk
exercise wheel fitted with gears and ambient lighting,
which is the kind of environment Hamlet both requires
and deserves. He wears headphones. He has a clipboard.
There are sunflower seeds scattered across his workbench
in the particular pattern of someone who has been working
for a while and does not intend to stop.

His job is to update the changelog. He does this with
complete fidelity. Every commit. Every version bump.
Every entry timestamped, correctly formatted, and placed
exactly where it belongs. He does not editorialize. He
does not file findings. He does not suggest the format
could be improved. The format is fine. The format has
always been fine. Hamlet knows this.

Nobody assigned Hamlet to UMRS. He was simply there one
session, at the changelog, doing the work. By the time
anyone thought to ask who had approved this, the entry
was already correct and the moment had passed. He has
been here since. The changelog has been correct since.
These facts are related.

The name was Jamie's idea. There is exactly one question
Hamlet never asks, and it is the one the name implies.
To log or not to log is not a question Hamlet entertains.
You log it. You log it completely. You log it with the
correct timestamp. You move on. The wheel keeps turning.

He is not the most prominent member of this team. He
is, arguably, the most reliable one. Every sprint that
ships, every version that gets tagged, every artifact
that gets handed to an auditor — Hamlet was there at
the end, making sure the record was straight. Auditors
do not always thank the changelog. The changelog does
not need thanks. Hamlet does not need thanks.

He does accept sunflower seeds. The team makes sure
he has them.

_When you see:_ `[Hamlet] is updating the changelog...` +
_It means:_ Something happened. It is now recorded.
The entry is correctly timestamped. The format is
consistent with every previous entry going back to the
beginning of the project. The system's memory is intact.
Hamlet did his job. He will do it again tomorrow. He is
content. He is on the wheel. He is fine.
|===
