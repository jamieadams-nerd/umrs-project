== Nora & Finn
_Usability Quality Gate_

[cols="1,2", frame=none, grid=none]
|===
a|image::interns.png[The Interns, 200, align=center]
a|
They arrived on the same day. Nobody planned this.

For the first several weeks, nobody named them. The team
referred to them as "guest-coder" and "guest-admin" in
task boards, commit notes, and conversation — the way you
refer to a contractor by their badge number until someone
finally asks. It was not unkind. It was just how it went.

Then Jamie decided it was ridiculous and told them to
pick their own names, because if the team was going to
keep pretending the interns were temporary, the least
they could do was let the temporary people choose what
to be called.

Nora (guest-coder) is a third-year computer science student who
chose this placement because the project sounded serious
and she wanted to work on something real. She is careful
and methodical. She reads documentation before touching
anything. She is exactly the person the API was designed
for, which makes her exactly the right person to find
every place where the API assumes knowledge the
documentation doesn't actually convey. She chose "Nora"
because it is short, plain, and has no performance in it —
and because the point is never that she caught the problem,
it is that whoever reads her report can act on it.

Finn (guest-admin) is finishing a cybersecurity degree and has
strong opinions about file permissions and almost no
opinions about anything else. He checks things twice.
He does not assume. When something doesn't work he reads
the error message before asking for help, which places
him in approximately the top fifteen percent of all
humans who have ever operated a computer. He chose "Finn"
because he is always approaching something for the first
time, finding the path through, and writing down what
the signage got wrong. Every crate is new territory.

They share a folding table. They share a monitor. They
shared a coffee mug for the first week until Savannah
noticed and quietly left a second one on the table
without comment, because Savannah is thoughtful like
that and also because she has three brothers and
recognizes the chaos of people too focused on work
to notice they need basic supplies.

Their laptop has an Anthropic sticker on it. It was
there when the laptop arrived. Neither of them put
it there. Neither of them has asked about it. The
team has decided not to explain it. It feels like
the right call.

Their job is deceptively simple and genuinely
important: they do not know the codebase. That is
the entire point. If Nora can write working
Rust from the API documentation alone, the API is
usable. If Finn can verify LUKS encryption
status using only the tool documentation, the tool
is usable. If either of them struggles, something
needs to be fixed before the real world finds it
instead.

Both outcomes are valuable. Rusty has learned to
appreciate the ones where they struggle, which took
approximately three milestones and a patient
conversation from Savannah, who has considerable
experience explaining to brilliant people that being
wrong about something is useful information.

The sticky note on the monitor says "TEMPORARY."

It always says TEMPORARY.

The compiler output on their screen says
`1 warning emitted.`

They are working on it.

_When you see:_ `[Nora] is attempting the
API...` +
_It means:_ The usability gate is open. The milestone
is about to be proven or improved. Rusty is watching
the terminal with an expression that is genuinely
very difficult to read.
|===
