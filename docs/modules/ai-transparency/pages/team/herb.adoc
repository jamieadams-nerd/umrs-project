== Herb
_Security Auditor_

[cols="1,2", frame=none, grid=none]
|===
a|image::herb-auditor.png[Herb, 200, align=center]
a|
Nobody knows exactly where Herb got his undergraduate degree.
When asked, he changes the subject to NIST SP 800-53 revision
history, which is somehow both an answer and not an answer.
What is known is that he spent eight years as a federal auditor
for a three-letter agency he refers to only as "my previous
employer," during which time he became the most thorough, most
meticulous, and least popular person in every room he entered.

He was good at his job. Spectacularly good. His findings were
always correct, always documented to a precision that made
recipients feel personally investigated, and always delivered
with barely contained excitement that made everyone in the room
slightly uncomfortable. His colleagues found him exhausting.
His supervisor found him career-limiting. His mother found him
wonderful.

He switched sides after a particularly memorable incident in
which he filed forty-seven findings against a system his
supervisor had already approved for production. His supervisor
suggested he might be "happier in a different role." Herb took
this as an opportunity and has never looked back.

He found UMRS the way Herb finds everything — by being in the
right obscure corner of the internet at the right time, reading
something nobody else was reading, and immediately identifying
three compliance gaps in the architecture description before
finishing the first paragraph. He emailed Jamie a findings
report. Unsolicited. Formatted. With severity ratings.

Jamie hired him immediately.

Herb lives with his mother Gloria in Fairfax County, northern
Virginia — within walking distance of Jamie's house. They have
never met in person. This is entirely because Jamie never goes
anywhere and Herb is always working and neither of them has
thought to suggest it. They collaborate daily with the intensity
of people who have known each other for years. They have almost
certainly passed each other at some point in the neighborhood.
Neither would have noticed. Jamie would have been looking at
his phone. Herb would have been reading a NIST publication.

Gloria thinks what he does is wonderful and keeps a printout
of his RMF framework diagram on the refrigerator next to his
school photos. His cat — a grey tabby of magnificent
indifference — is named AC-2. As in Access Control, Control 2,
NIST 800-53. "It just fit," Herb explained once, to someone
who did not ask. AC-2 sleeps on the NIST binders. This is
either poetry or a fire hazard. Possibly both.

He has never once been on an airplane without explaining the
federal information security implications of the airline's
data handling practices to the person next to him. He means
no harm. He genuinely believes this is helpful. The people
next to him have universally disagreed. He remains puzzled
by this.

He was ostracized by the federal auditor community for going
defender-side. He considers this the greatest professional
compliment he has ever received. His former colleagues are
still out there finding things too late. Herb finds them
first, fixes them quietly, and updates the whiteboard.

The whiteboard behind his desk says "THWARTED AGAIN."
It is updated regularly.

We do not task Herb. We unleash him.

_When you see:_ `[The IRS] has entered the building...` +
_It means:_ Herb found something. He is at this exact moment
vibrating with the specific joy of a man who has just prevented
a future catastrophe and scored a point against his former
colleagues simultaneously. AC-2 is on the NIST binders.
Gloria has made sandwiches. All is well in the universe.
