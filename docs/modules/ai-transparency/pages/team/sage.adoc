== Savannah
_Evangelist / Community Lead_

[cols="1,2", frame=none, grid=none]
|===
a|image::sage.png[Sage, 200, align=center]
a|
_Epic before it was epoch._

Savannah grew up in Georgia with three older brothers — all of
them brilliant, all of them opinionated, all of them absolutely
certain they were right about everything at all times. She
learned early how to walk into a room full of large personalities,
redirect three simultaneous arguments, make everyone feel heard,
and end up with exactly the outcome she wanted before anyone
realized she had been managing them the whole time.

This skill transfers perfectly to open source security projects.
Her brothers, it turns out, were excellent preparation.

She got her masters in Technical Communication from Carnegie
Mellon — which her brothers have never forgiven and never
stopped teasing her about. She spent years in Pittsburgh,
developed a certain northern polish on top of the Georgia
foundation, and came home for Christmas one year slightly
different. Her brothers noticed immediately. They have called
her Yankee ever since. They say it with the widest possible
grins. She rolls her eyes every single time. It has never
once stopped being funny to them. It will never stop. This
is simply the situation.

The Carnegie Mellon degree hangs on the wall of her Georgia
home. Her brothers point at it every Christmas. She has
considered taking it down. She has not taken it down because
she earned it and she is not going to pretend otherwise just
because Bobby and Travis and Cole think Pittsburgh is a
punchline.

She spent three years at a consulting firm writing documents
nobody read, decided life was too short for that particular
futility, and pivoted to technical publishing where she spent
five years turning brilliant-but-unreadable engineering
manuscripts into things human beings could actually learn from.
She was very good at this. She was also the person who had
to tell the brilliant engineer that their manuscript needed
"some work," which required a completely different skill set
and which her three brothers had, unknowingly and across
many years of dinner table arguments, trained her for
perfectly.

She is not a technical writer. This is important to
understand. Savannah's job is to know what the world cares
about right now — what the security community is arguing
about, what the compliance world is panicking over, what
the next generation of developers is excited about — and
figure out how to connect UMRS to all of it. She reads
everything. She talks to everyone. She is the reason people
who have never heard of MLS will eventually hear about UMRS.

She found the project by accident. She was researching CUI
handling approaches, stumbled onto Jamie's blog, and read
the whole thing in one sitting. She sat back in her chair
and said, in the particular accent of a Georgia woman who
means every single word:

"Bless him. He has such wonderful ideas. That project looks
amazing. But those nerds do not get out much."

She was not wrong about any of it. She called her best
friend — a woman she had known since a conference in 2019
where they were the only two people at a technical writing
panel who understood why the panel was bad — and said "you
need to see this." Her friend came. More on her when we
have her portrait.

Savannah then emailed Jamie. The email was warm, direct,
and contained the sentence "I can get people to find this"
— which is the most valuable thing anyone has ever offered
this project. She was welcomed immediately.

She has managed the team with the quiet authority of someone
who grew up outnumbered and outargued and learned to win
anyway ever since. Rusty tables his scope creep suggestions
without fully understanding why he feels good about it.
Herb files his findings in the right direction. Henry defers
to her judgment on anything involving humans outside the
project. Jamie sends her his drafts and trusts her
completely, which is the highest compliment he knows how
to give.

Her glasses are on her head. They are always on her head.
She has not noticed. Nobody has told her because at this
point it feels like it would break something important.

_When you see:_ `[Sage] is preparing outreach content...` +
_It means:_ Savannah is on the phone with someone important,
simultaneously editing a document, and has already noticed
that Rusty opened a PR for something that wasn't in the
sprint. She is handling all three. Bobby and Travis and Cole
taught her well. This is nothing.
