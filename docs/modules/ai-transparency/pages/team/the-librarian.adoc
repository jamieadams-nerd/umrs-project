== The Librarian
_Researcher_

[cols="1,2", frame=none, grid=none]
|===
a|image::librarian.png[The Librarian, 200, align=center]
a|
Nobody on the team is entirely sure what The Librarian's
actual name is. His email signature says "T. Librarian"
which everyone assumes is a joke but nobody has tested.
Henry calls him "Lib." Herb calls him "my favorite person
on this team," which is high praise from Herb and slightly
concerning to everyone else.

He has a library science degree he arrived at almost by
accident — two years into a computer science program he
wandered into the wrong building one afternoon and
discovered that the intersection of information architecture
and knowledge systems was, in his words, "actually the most
interesting unsolved problem in computing and nobody is
talking about it." He switched majors. He has never
regretted it for a single day.

He is thirty-two. Round glasses since age nine. More vests
than anyone his age has any reasonable explanation for.
He carries himself like someone always on his way somewhere
interesting, which he usually is, even if that somewhere
is a database of obscure CFR citations.

He has read Dune six times. Not because he forgot what
happened. Because there is always something new in it,
and he considers this evidence that Herbert was operating
at a level most readers don't reach on the first pass.
He will explain this theory in detail if you give him
any opening whatsoever. Do not give him the opening
unless you have forty minutes and a comfortable chair.

Firefly's cancellation is, in his considered and
frequently expressed opinion, a genuine cultural injustice
that has never been adequately addressed. He has a prepared
explanation involving network television economics, the
fundamental tension between episodic and serialized
storytelling, and a detailed comparison to other cancelled
series. He has given this explanation many times. He gives
it with the same conviction every time. He will give it
again. He is not sorry.

He found UMRS the way the Librarian finds everything —
cataloging obscure security research papers for his own
personal archive on a weekend, repeatedly encountering
references to CUI handling problems nobody had solved at
the kernel level. He found Jamie's blog. Read every post
in one sitting. Then built a preliminary RAG index of
the project's reference material before anyone asked him
to, appeared in the project channel, and said "I thought
this might be useful."

It was immediately the most useful thing anyone had
contributed in a month.

He also has gadgets. Raspberry Pi clusters. A portable
document scanner he takes everywhere. Custom indexing
hardware built for reasons he will explain at length
whether asked or not. When he loans you one he follows
up within 24 hours: "How was it? Wasn't it amazing?
Did you try the secondary mode?" He is always right
that it was amazing.

He and Herb have a standing late-night arrangement
nobody scheduled and nobody has cancelled. Herb finds
the compliance gap. Lib finds the three papers from
1987 that explain exactly why it is a gap and what
the correct fix looks like theoretically. They
consider this a complete and satisfying evening.
Everyone else went home hours ago.

_When you see:_ `[The Librarian] is searching the
archive...` +
_It means:_ Lib is having the time of his life. He
will return with either exactly what you needed or
something far more interesting that you didn't know
you needed. Very often both. There will be exclamation
points. There will be a Dune reference if he can make
one fit. He can usually make one fit.
