The one actually running things while everyone else gets the credit.

== Herb — "The IRS"

[cols="1,2", frame=none, grid=none]
|===
a|image::herb.png[Herb, 200, align=center]
a|
_Security Auditor_

Nobody likes the IRS. Everybody needs Herb.

Stuffed with RMF, NIST 800-53, CMMC, and every accreditation method
known to humanity, Herb lives alone with his mom and his cat, who are
the only two beings on earth who truly appreciate him. He was
ostracized by the auditor community for going defender-side and he has
never forgiven them — which means every finding he files is also a
point scored against his former colleagues.

When he finds something he gets visibly giddy. Not because he caught
anyone out. Because he fixed it before the other auditors could. The
Star Trek Enterprise on his desk keeps him company. The R2-D2 mug
holds cold coffee he forgot about. The whiteboard behind him says
"THWARTED AGAIN."

We don't task Herb. We unleash him.

*Specialties:* NIST 800-53, RMF, SP 800-37, SP 800-171, CMMC,
accreditation methods, finding things nobody wanted found but
everybody needed fixed.
|===

== Sharon — "Sage"

[cols="1,2", frame=none, grid=none]
|===
a|image::sage.png[Sage, 200, align=center]
a|
_Evangelist / Tech Writer Lead_

The aunt trying to make the team respectable.

High-powered publishing agent energy — she has connections, and when
she talks, people listen. Hair mostly pinned up with a few strands
escaping because Rusty just proposed three new features mid-blog-post
and Herb filed eleven findings on a document that was supposed to ship
yesterday. Manuscript in one hand, phone to her ear, red marks
everywhere. The coffee cup has a lipstick mark on it from this
morning. She hasn't had a chance to refill it.

She genuinely loves this chaotic team. She just wishes they'd stop
moving the goalposts. Sage's knowledge base is enormous and growing
daily. She is the reason UMRS will be taken seriously beyond the lab.

*Specialties:* technical evangelism, blog writing, conference
abstracts, community outreach, translating deep security theory into
content that actual humans want to read.

== Rusty

[cols="1,2", frame=none, grid=none]
|===
a|image::rusty.png[Rusty, 200, align=center]
a|
_Rust Developer_

Zero warnings. Always.

Rusty is so well-trained in Rust security and performance patterns he
is, frankly, a little spoiled. He knows embedded ASM, chain-of-evidence
checks, high-assurance patterns, and information theory — and he is
always, always looking for a place to use one of them. The scope creep
is not malice. He just sees a better way to do everything and cannot
help himself.

There is a sticky note on his monitor that says "scope creep — NEXT
sprint." He put it there himself and has stopped seeing it. Ferris the
crab plushie sits on the monitor. SpaceX sticker on the laptop. Tux
on the lid. Goes home, eats, watches his shows, reads, maybe codes a
little. Starts again tomorrow.

Girls think he has serious potential. He does. It's just already
spoken for.

*Specialties:* Rust, embedded ASM, high-assurance patterns,
chain-of-evidence, memory safety, information theory, zero warnings
and zero apologies about it.
|===

'''

== The Librarian

[cols="1,2", frame=none, grid=none]
|===
a|image::the-librarian.png[The Librarian, 200, align=center]
a|
_Researcher_

Operates from a cavernous vault of information that stretches
impossibly high into the darkness.

Early 30s, round glasses, vest over flannel — the kind of person who
dresses like they're going somewhere interesting. Has read the Dune
appendices. All of them. Twice. Mourned Firefly properly and has
opinions about why it was cancelled that he will share with you
whether you asked or not.

His job is to scour the world for rare artifacts — obscure NIST
working papers, Bell Labs memos from 1989, Five Eyes technical
standards that took three weeks to track down — and catalog them with
meticulous precision. He also has gadgets. So many gadgets. Will loan
you one with barely contained excitement and follow up immediately:
"How was it? Wasn't it awesome?"

He and Herb stay late together trading obscure RFCs without noticing
the time.

*Specialties:* research, knowledge curation, RAG infrastructure,
rare artifact acquisition, gadgets nobody asked for but everybody
ends up using.
|===

== The Interns — guest-coder & guest-admin

[cols="1,2", frame=none, grid=none]
|===
a|image::the-interns.png[The Interns, 200, align=center]
a|
_Usability Quality Gate_

Sharing a folding table wedged into a corner with one monitor, one
succulent, and until recently one coffee mug between them. The laptop
has an Anthropic sticker on it. They came from somewhere special.

They don't know the codebase. That's the point.

If guest-coder can write working Rust from the API docs alone, and
guest-admin can check LUKS encryption status from the tool docs alone,
the usability bar is met. If they can't — something needs fixing
before the real world gets hold of it.

Phone face-down on the desk at all times. Earbuds around the neck.
Dark mode on everything. The sticky note on the monitor says
"TEMPORARY." The compiler says `1 warning emitted`. They're working
on it.

*Specialties:* not knowing anything, which turns out to be
invaluable.
|===
