2. The Real Problem: Parisian French ≠ Canadian French

This is where most systems quietly fail.

Key differences:
	•	Vocabulary (e.g., “courriel” vs “email” nuance, “char” vs “voiture”)
	•	Tone (Canadian French tends to be more direct and practical)
	•	Bureaucratic phrasing differs significantly
	•	Anglicisms are handled differently
	•	Instructional clarity expectations are different

👉 Translation engines default heavily toward France French (fr-FR) unless corrected.

3. Corpus Strategy (This Is the Critical Piece)

You’re absolutely right: the agent must read a corpus.

You want authoritative, real-world Canadian French usage, not generic text.

High-Value Corpus Sources

🇨🇦 Government of Canada (Gold Standard)
	•	Government of Canada official publications
	•	Look for:
	•	“GC Style Guide”
	•	Treasury Board documentation
	•	security / compliance docs
	•	bilingual pages (EN/FR side-by-side)

Why this matters:
	•	This is exactly your domain tone (structured, compliance-heavy)
	•	Terminology is standardized and vetted
