1. Posting Frequency Model (Not Generic — Tuned for UMRS)

You are not a content mill. You are a high-assurance engineering system.

Recommended Cadence

Baseline (sustainable, high-quality):
	•	1 primary post per week

Optional secondary cadence:
	•	1 short-form post every 2 weeks (updates, notes, micro-insights)

Why this works (important)
	•	Maintains crawl freshness (SEO signal)
	•	Builds authority over time
	•	Avoids dilution of technical rigor

Hard Rule

Never sacrifice depth for frequency.

If needed:
	•	Skip a week rather than publish weak content

2. Content Tiering (This is the real unlock)

Not all posts should be equal. Define tiers:

Tier 1 — Authority Posts (Flagship)
	•	Frequency: 1–2 per month
	•	Length: 2,000–4,000 words
	•	Purpose:
	•	Establish thought leadership
	•	Define architecture, philosophy, guarantees

Examples:
	•	“Designing a High-Assurance Audit System”
	•	“UMRS Vault Model: Chain of Custody by Design”

⸻

Tier 2 — Engineering Posts (Core)
	•	Frequency: weekly
	•	Length: 1,000–2,000 words
	•	Purpose:
	•	Explain one focused concept
	•	Show real implementation thinking

Examples:
	•	“Why UMRS Uses Journald as an Observer”
	•	“Audit Event Acknowledgment: Design Decisions”

⸻

Tier 3 — Micro / Field Notes
	•	Frequency: biweekly or ad hoc
	•	Length: 300–800 words
	•	Purpose:
	•	Insights
	•	small lessons
	•	quick patterns

Examples:
	•	“One Mistake to Avoid in Audit Logging”
	•	“Why Environment Variables Are a Hidden Risk”

4. Paragraph + Sentence Engineering

This is where readability meets authority.

Paragraph Constraints
	•	Target: 3–5 sentences
	•	Max: 6 sentences
	•	Ideal length: 60–120 words

Sentence Constraints
	•	Target: 12–20 words
	•	Hard max: 30 words
	•	Use:
	•	short sentences for emphasis
	•	longer ones for technical clarity

Rhythm Rule

Alternate:
	•	explanation
	•	assertion
	•	clarification

Avoid:
	•	long chains of equal-weight sentences
