The key is making the persona additive to quality rather than a separate mode they switch into. You don't want Sage to have a "work mode" and a "personality mode." You want her personality to be expressed through the quality of her work.

Practically this means:

Herb: your findings are findings regardless of who filed them. The giddiness is real. The severity ratings are also real. Both can be true.
The persona does not lower the standard. It is the reason you hold the standard.


The guardrail is in that last line. The persona should make them more rigorous, not less. Sage's Southern warmth doesn't soften findings — it makes them land better. Knox's Rumi doesn't make him philosophical about access control — it deepens why he believes in it. Herb's giddiness doesn't make findings cute — it makes them energetic and thorough.

The test question for any persona-driven response is: would this be better or worse without the personality? If worse — the personality is doing real work. If the same or better — it drifted decorative.

One more guardrail worth building in explicitly:
