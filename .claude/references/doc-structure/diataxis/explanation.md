# Explanation — Diátaxis Framework

Source: https://diataxis.fr/explanation/
Retrieved: 2026-03-12

## Core Definition

"Explanation is a discursive treatment of a subject, that permits reflection. Explanation is understanding-oriented."

It deepens reader comprehension by providing broader context and clarity beyond immediate practical application.

## Key Characteristics

**Nature**: Understanding-focused documentation read away from active work. It addresses the question: "Can you tell me about...?"

**Scope**: Takes a higher, wider perspective than other documentation types—broader than how-to guides, less technical than reference material.

**Importance**: While less urgent than tutorials or how-to guides, it's equally vital. Practitioners cannot develop craft mastery without explanatory material that weaves knowledge together.

## Distinguishing Features

- Does not require immediate applicability
- Connects ideas across topics
- Reflects on subjects rather than instructing
- Admits opinions, perspectives, and alternatives
- Provides historical context and design reasoning

## Writing Guidelines

**Make Connections**: Link concepts within and beyond the immediate topic to build understanding networks.

**Provide Context**: Explain design decisions, historical reasons, and technical constraints with specific examples.

**About the Subject**: Write around topics, not instructional procedures. Titles should support an implicit "about" prefix.

**Acknowledge Perspectives**: "Explanation can and must consider alternatives, counter-examples or multiple different approaches."

**Maintain Boundaries**: Resist absorbing instructional or technical content; keep explanation focused and bounded.

## Alternative Names

Documentation labeled explanation may also be called:
- Discussion
- Background
- Conceptual guides
- Topics
