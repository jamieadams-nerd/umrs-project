# How-to Guides — Diátaxis Framework

Source: https://diataxis.fr/how-to-guides/
Retrieved: 2026-03-12

## Overview

"How-to guides are **directions** that guide the reader through a problem or towards a result.
How-to guides are **goal-oriented**."

These practical documents help users accomplish specific tasks correctly and safely. They focus on "_work_ - navigating from one side to the other of a real-world problem-field."

Examples include calibrating radar arrays or configuring reconnection policies. Comprehensive how-to guide collections demonstrate a product's capabilities to potential users.

## Core Principle: User-Centered, Problem-Focused

"**How-to guides must be written from the perspective of the user, not of the machinery.**"
They represent genuine needs rather than showcasing features for their own sake.

Ineffective guides merely describe tool operations without addressing purpose. Strong guides answer real human questions about achieving desired outcomes with available tools.

## Distinguishing Characteristics

### What How-to Guides Are NOT

- Not tutorials: they serve different user needs
- Not purely procedural: real problems rarely follow linear paths
- Not complete references: practical usefulness trumps comprehensiveness

### Key Principles

**Focused content**: Concentrate exclusively on the stated goal—avoid explanations, teaching moments, and tangential discussions.

**Adaptability**: Address real-world complexity by remaining flexible enough for users to customize guidance to their specific situations.

**Practical brevity**: Start and end at meaningful points; readers supply their own context.

**Executable solutions**: Provide actionable steps including both physical actions and thinking processes.

**Logical sequencing**: Order steps according to natural dependencies and cognitive flow.

**Anticipatory flow**: Structure guidance to feel like a helper providing tools before the reader needs them.

## Naming Conventions

Titles should clearly state deliverables:
- Strong: "How to integrate application performance monitoring"
- Weak: "Application performance monitoring" (ambiguous purpose)

## Language Patterns

Effective how-to guides employ conditional imperatives: "If you want x, do y." They reference external materials rather than embedding unnecessary reference content.

## The Recipe Model

Recipes exemplify ideal how-to structure. They define specific outcomes, address targeted questions, require baseline competence, and exclude teaching and historical context. Format consistency helps readers navigate efficiently toward results.
