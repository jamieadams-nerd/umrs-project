# Voice and Tone — Google Developer Documentation Style Guide

Source: https://developers.google.com/style/tone
Retrieved: 2026-03-12

## Page Summary

- Write conversationally and respectfully, avoiding slang and overly casual language
- Prioritize clear, straightforward language for global audiences with varying English proficiency
- Avoid jargon, clichés, and culturally specific references
- Maintain natural, engaging tone while keeping information delivery as the primary focus
- Be polite without overusing "please" in instructions

## Overview

The guide emphasizes adopting "a conversational, friendly, and respectful tone that is approachable and easy to understand." The voice should sound "like a knowledgeable friend who understands what the developer wants to do."

Documentation should be conversational rather than formal, but not excessively verbose or colloquial. "Be human, let your personality show, and be memorable," while remembering that readers seeking information may be in a hurry.

## Things to Avoid

- Buzzwords or technical jargon
- Excessive cuteness or wackiness
- Ableist language or problematic figures of speech
- Placeholder phrases ("please note," "at this time")
- Choppy or unnecessarily long sentences
- Repetitive sentence structures
- Pop culture references
- Exclamation marks (use sparingly)
- Condescending language like "simply" or "it's easy"
- Internet slang and abbreviations
- Denigrating language toward any group

## Politeness Guidelines

"It's great to be polite, but using please in a set of instructions is overdoing the politeness."

**Recommended:** "To view the document, click View."

**Not recommended:** "To view the document, please click View."

## Practical Techniques

- Step back and ask yourself what you're trying to communicate
- Seek colleague feedback on uncertain phrasing
- Read sections aloud to assess naturalness
- Use transitions between sentences for flow
- Prioritize clarity and usefulness above all else

## Examples Comparing Tone Levels

| Too Informal | Just Right | Too Formal |
|---|---|---|
| "Dude! This API is totally awesome!" | "This API lets you collect data about what your users like." | "The API documented by this page may enable the acquisition of information pertaining to user preferences." |
| "Just like a pop star, get your telephone number easily!" | "To get the user's phone number, call `user.phoneNumber.get`." | "The telephone number can be retrieved via the simple expedient of using the `get` method..." |
