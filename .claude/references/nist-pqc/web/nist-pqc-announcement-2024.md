# NIST Releases First 3 Finalized Post-Quantum Encryption Standards

**Source URL:** https://www.nist.gov/news-events/news/2024/08/nist-releases-first-3-finalized-post-quantum-encryption-standards
**Published:** August 13, 2024
**Issuing authority:** National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST)
**Retrieved:** 2026-03-13

---

## Overview

The U.S. Department of Commerce's National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) has finalized encryption algorithms designed to resist cyberattacks from quantum computers. These three standards are ready for immediate use and represent the culmination of an eight-year effort.

## Key Points

**Timeline and Urgency:**
The standards address an emerging threat. Some experts predict that quantum computers capable of breaking current encryption could appear within a decade, threatening security for individuals, organizations, and nations.

**Official Statement:**
According to Deputy Secretary of Commerce Don Graves, "The advancement of quantum computing plays an essential role in reaffirming America's status as a global technological powerhouse."

NIST Director Laurie E. Locascio emphasized that "these finalized standards are the capstone of NIST's efforts to safeguard our confidential electronic information."

## The Three Finalized Standards

### FIPS 203 (ML-KEM)
- Primary standard for general encryption
- Based on CRYSTALS-Kyber algorithm
- Advantages include small encryption keys and fast operation
- Module-Lattice-Based Key-Encapsulation Mechanism

### FIPS 204 (ML-DSA)
- Primary standard for digital signatures
- Based on CRYSTALS-Dilithium algorithm
- Module-Lattice-Based Digital Signature Algorithm

### FIPS 205 (SLH-DSA)
- Secondary standard for digital signatures
- Based on Sphincs+ algorithm
- Stateless Hash-Based Digital Signature Algorithm
- Serves as backup using different mathematical approach (hash-based vs. lattice-based)

## Implementation

NIST mathematician Dustin Moody stated that "full integration will take time," encouraging system administrators to begin incorporating these standards immediately.

## Future Work

NIST continues evaluating two additional algorithm sets for potential backup standards, with selections expected by end of 2024 and ongoing evaluation of additional digital signature algorithms. FIPS 206 (FN-DSA, based on FALCON) is under development.
