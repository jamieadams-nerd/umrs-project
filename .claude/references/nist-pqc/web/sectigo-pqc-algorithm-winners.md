## Key Points from Search Excerpt

NIST is leading the charge for standardizing quantum-resistant algorithms. In 2023, NIST announced the first four post-quantum algorithm winners. On August 13, 2024, they released the first three FIPS PQC standards:

- **FIPS 203** (ML-KEM) — Module-Lattice-Based Key-Encapsulation Mechanism, derived from CRYSTALS-Kyber
- **FIPS 204** (ML-DSA) — Module-Lattice-Based Digital Signature Standard, derived from CRYSTALS-Dilithium
- **FIPS 205** (SLH-DSA) — Stateless Hash-Based Digital Signature Standard, derived from SPHINCS+

## Algorithm Replacement Context

Sectigo, as a certificate authority, covers these standards in the context of PKI migration:

- RSA-based certificates will need migration to ML-DSA (FIPS 204) or SLH-DSA (FIPS 205)
- ECDSA-based certificates will need migration to ML-DSA (FIPS 204) or SLH-DSA (FIPS 205)
- Key exchange in TLS will need migration to ML-KEM (FIPS 203)
- Post-quantum certificates anticipated in TLS/WebPKI ecosystem around 2026
