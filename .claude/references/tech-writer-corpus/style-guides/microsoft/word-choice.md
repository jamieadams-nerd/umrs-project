## Use Simple Words, Concise Sentences

**Source:** https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/style-guide/word-choice/use-simple-words-concise-sentences
**Retrieved:** 2026-03-16

Make every word count. Concise, clear sentences save space, are easy to understand, and facilitate
scanning. Use simple words with precise meanings, and remove words that don't add substance. Use
your judgment to avoid sounding abrupt or unfriendly.

### Choose simple verbs without modifiers

Whenever you can, avoid weak or vague verbs, such as *be, have, make,* and *do.*

| Use this | Not this |
|----------|----------|
| use | utilize, make use of |
| remove | extract, take away, eliminate |
| tell | inform, let know |

### Don't use two or three words when one will do

| Use this | Not this |
|----------|----------|
| to | in order to, as a means to |
| also | in addition |
| connect | establish connectivity |

### Choose words that have one clear meaning

| Use this | Not this |
|----------|----------|
| *Because* you created the table, you can change it. | *Since* you created the table, you can change it. |

### Omit unnecessary adverbs

Unless they're important to the meaning of a statement, leave them out. Examples of adverbs to
omit: quite, very, quickly, easily, effectively.

### Handle dual-use words carefully

Use words that can be both nouns and verbs carefully—*file, post, mark, screen, record,* and
*report,* for example. Use the sentence structure and context to eliminate ambiguity.
