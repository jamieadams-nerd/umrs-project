# Microsoft Writing Style Guide — Top 10 Tips for Style and Voice

**Source:** https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/style-guide/top-10-tips-style-voice
**Retrieved:** 2026-03-16
**Last updated (source):** 2025-01-21

---

## 1. Use bigger ideas, fewer words

Our modern design hinges on crisp minimalism. Shorter is always better.

**Example**
- Replace: "If you're ready to purchase Office 365 for your organization, contact your Microsoft account representative."
- With: "Ready to buy? Contact us."

## 2. Write like you speak

Write like you speak. Read your text aloud. Avoid jargon and overly complex or technical language.
It should sound like a friendly conversation.

**Example**
- Replace: "Invalid ID"
- With: "You need an ID that looks like this: someone@example.com"

## 3. Project friendliness

Use contractions: *it's, you'll, you're, we're, let's.*

**Example**
- Replace: "...know what you are interested in, what is on your calendar..."
- With: "...know what you're interested in, what's on your calendar..."

## 4. Get to the point fast

Lead with what's most important. Front-load keywords for scanning. Make customer choices and
next steps obvious.

**Example**
- Replace: "Templates provide a starting point for creating new documents. A template can include
  the styles, formats, and page layouts you use frequently. Consider creating a template if you
  often use the same page layout and style for documents."
- With: "Save time by creating a document template that includes the styles, formats, and page
  layouts you use most often. Then use the template whenever you create a new document."

## 5. Be brief

Give customers just enough information to make decisions confidently. Prune every excess word.

**Example**
- Replace: "The Recommended Charts command on the Insert tab recommends charts that are likely to
  represent your data well. Use the command when you want to visually present data, and you're not
  sure how to do it."
- With: "Create a chart that's just right for your data by using the Recommended Charts command on
  the Insert tab."

## 6. When in doubt, don't capitalize

Default to sentence-style capitalization—capitalize only the first word of a heading or phrase and
any proper nouns or names. Never Use Title Capitalization (Like This). Never Ever.

**Examples**
- Replace: "Find a Microsoft Partner", "Office 365 Customer", "Limited-Time Offer"
- With: "Find a Microsoft partner", "Office 365 customer", "Limited-time offer"

## 7. Skip periods (and : ! ?)

Skip end punctuation on titles, headings, subheadings, UI titles, and items in a list that are
three or fewer words. Save the periods for paragraphs and body copy.

**Example**
- Replace: "**Move a tile.** 1. Press and hold the tile."
- With: "**Move a tile** 1. Press and hold the tile."

## 8. Remember the last comma

In a list of three or more items, include a comma before the conjunction. (The Oxford or serial
comma.)

**Example**
- Replace: "Android, iOS and Windows"
- With: "Android, iOS, and Windows"

## 9. Don't be spacey

Use only one space after periods, question marks, and colons—and no spaces around dashes.

**Example**
- Replace: "Use pipelines — logical groups of activities — to consolidate..."
- With: "Use pipelines—logical groups of activities—to consolidate..."

## 10. Revise weak writing

Most of the time, start each statement with a verb. Edit out *you can* and *there is, there are,
there were.*

**Example**
- Replace: "You can access Office apps across your devices, and you get online file storage and sharing."
- With: "Store files online, access them from all your devices, and share them with coworkers."
