## Documents

### Miller — Genre as Social Action (1984)
- **File:** `miller-genre-as-social-action.pdf`
- **Source:** https://oportuguesdobrasil.files.wordpress.com/2015/02/miller_genre_as_social_action.pdf
- **Author:** Carolyn Miller
- **Published:** Quarterly Journal of Speech, Vol. 70, 1984
- **Description:** The most-cited article in technical communication. Defines genre as typified rhetorical action, connecting Bitzer's rhetorical situation to Schutz's typification theory. This is the foundational move that made technical communication an academic discipline. Explains why a deployment guide, a reference page, and a blog post are fundamentally different genres with different social functions.
- **License:** Author-distributed open access

### Bazerman — Genre as Social Action (2011)
- **File:** `bazerman-genre-as-social-action.pdf`
- **Source:** https://bazerman.education.ucsb.edu/sites/default/files/docs/Bazerman2011chapGenreassocialaction.pdf
- **Author:** Charles Bazerman
- **Published:** Chapter in Routledge Handbook of Discourse Analysis, 2011
- **Description:** Bazerman's extension of Miller's genre framework. Traces genre from speech act theory through activity theory. Explains how genres coordinate social action in communities of practice. Directly applicable to understanding why UMRS documentation genres (deployment guide, audit report, reference page) have different success criteria.
- **License:** Author-distributed from UCSB faculty page
