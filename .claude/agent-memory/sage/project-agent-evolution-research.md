# Agent Evolution Research — Jamie's Parallel AI Study

**Created:** 2026-03-22
**Priority:** HIGH — Jamie considers this equally important to UMRS technical content

## What This Is

Jamie is building a research case that a small team of deeply knowledge-trained agents
outperforms large swarms (80+) of generic agents. This is a parallel track alongside
UMRS — not secondary to it.

The core thesis: **knowledge acquisition produces qualitative capability shifts, not
quantitative ones.** You don't get "more findings" — you get fundamentally different
kinds of findings. And critically, the RAG + familiarization architecture creates an
**audit trail of intellectual development** that you can trace to specific dates,
documents, and behavioral changes. That traceability is the differentiator.

## Evidence Produced So Far

### Herb Evolution Study (2026-03-22)
- **Full report:** `.claude/reports/herb-evolution-study-2026-03-22.md`
- **Quick reference:** `.claude/jamies_brain/agent-evolution-highlights.md`
- **Copy in Jamie's brain:** `.claude/jamies_brain/herb-evolution-study.md`

**Key finding:** Three inflection points traced to specific corpus ingestion events:
1. RMF corpus (3/15) → Herb shifted from "is this secure?" to "can an assessor use this?"
2. SCAP/STIG (3/17-18) → Generic NIST citations became CCE-specific work instructions
3. Convergence (3/20-21) → Full assessment-value lens as default operating mode

The strongest evidence: the 3/15 portfolio-level observation ("strong Implement, weak
Assess/Monitor") could not have existed before the RMF familiarization. That's a
capability that was created by knowledge, not by prompting.
